Johnson Wax Professional (JWP) is a global marketer of products and solutions for sanitation, cleaning and maintenance professionals. JWP helps Cleanwise customers tackle their wide range of jan/san needs - from floor care, carpet care, restroom and laundry care, to chemical management, pest control, cleaners and disinfectants. The company operates as an independent subsidiary of S. C. Johnson & Son, Inc.

3M is a $15.7 billion manufacturing and technology powerhouse with a wide range of products, serving dozens of markets including industrial, health care, transportation safety, consumer, and office, among others. With an entire product line devoted to facility cleaning and care – from fabric protectors, adhesives, and grouts, to flooring and chemical management systems, 3M products help major corporations worldwide keep their buildings clean and safe.

Himolene is a pioneer in the area of commercial waste disposal products, having been the first company to produce high-density, polyethylene (HDPE) can liners in the US. The company is a division of the $4 billion Clorox Company.

Kimberly-Clark is the world’s leading tissue manufacturer responsible for such global commercial and consumer brands as Kleenex, Scott, Kimberly-Clark, Kimwipes and Wypall. The company prides itself on providing quality, innovative products and technology leadership focused on three global businesses: tissue, personal care, and health care. As a Fortune 500 company, Kimberly-Clark generates more than $12 billion in annual sales, and has been named one of Fortune magazine’s “Most Admired” corporations since 1983.

NSS Enterprises, Inc. has been serving commercial industries since 1911 as the only owner-operated, full-line commercial floor maintenance and carpet care equipment company in the world. With over 600 distributors worldwide, NSS customers can be found in over 60 countries, and include such high-profile names as American Airlines, Hilton Hotels, K-Mart, Sun Healthcare, and the “Big Three” automakers.

Rubbermaid is a $7 billion global leader in the manufacturing and marketing of plastic and rubber products. Rubbermaid Commercial Products (RCP), headquartered in Winchester, Virginia, was established in 1967 to meet commercial and institutional cleaning needs with products in such categories as food service, sanitary maintenance, waste handling, and material transport. In 1999, Rubbermaid, Inc. merged with the Newell Co. and formed Newell Rubbermaid, Inc. Today, Rubbermaid Commercial Products is a division of Newell Rubbermaid Inc.
