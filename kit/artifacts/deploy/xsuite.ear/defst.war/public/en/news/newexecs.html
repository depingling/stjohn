New Executives Bring Wealth of Leadership and Technology Skills to the Cleanwise Team

MARLBOROUGH, Mass., March 5 -- Cleanwise, a company dedicated to consolidating and streamlining the janitorial/sanitary (jan/san) supply chain, today announced the addition of Ken Mawhinney, vice president of product and development, and Rob Urbani, vice president of professional services, to its management team. These two hires further strengthen Cleanwise's position as a leading provider of online products, services, and expertise for the jan/san industry.

In his new role, Mawhinney directs the future progression of both the cleanwise.com and eStores products, handling all technology implementation and use. Urbani leads the professional services division, supporting the sales initiatives and adding value to Cleanwise customers through implementation and benefit programs.

As vice president of product and development, Mawhinney will ensure that both cleanwise.com and eStores are the leading Internet resources for the jan/san industry, and that they will continue to evolve as new technology becomes available. Most recently, Mawhinney was a software engineer at Open Market, where he helped develop successive releases of its flagship Electronic commerce system, Transact. Prior to Open Market, Mawhinney was a senior development engineer for Raytheon and the technical lead on the Airport Surface Detection Equipment project. In this capacity, his responsibilities included: selection of the development tools; build and configuration management; the installation plan; and, the development of test drivers to verify the hardware and software against requirements. Additionally at Raytheon, Mawhinney was a software group leader for the upgrade to Flylingsdale's Ballistic Missile Early Warning System and the Canadian Government's Radar Modernization Project. Mawhinney has also worked for Marconi Defense Systems and the New University of Ulster. He has his B.S. from the University of St. Andrews and his Ph.D. in Physics from New University of Ulster.

Urbani oversees Cleanwise Professional Services, putting to work his 15 years of business strategy and e-commerce consulting experience working with start-up companies, and improving the sales and marketing efforts of large technology companies. Most recently, Urbani was a director in the solution provider practice at Surgency, Inc. While there, he worked with several multi-billion dollar global technology providers to drive their strategy development around e-commerce initiatives, and created sales and knowledge management tools for communicating their unique value propositions and increasing their sales effectiveness. He also helped several start-up companies develop business plans, value propositions, and partner and alliance strategies. Prior to working at Surgency, Urbani was a strategic services consulting manager at KPMG, where he managed corporate re-engineering, strategy, and systems implementation projects for several large enterprise clients. He also held senior consulting positions at Factory Mutual Engineering & Research and Ernst & Young. Urbani earned his B.S. in Manufacturing Engineering from Boston University.

"Ken and Rob bring an exceptional combination of business and technical expertise to our management team," said Keith Alsheimer, president of Cleanwise. "Their addition demonstrates our commitment to providing our customers, and the jan/san industry as a whole, with the best service possible, while also ensuring we use the most advanced technology to do so."

About Cleanwise
Cleanwise provides the tools and technologies to deliver superior results to manufacturers, distributors, and enterprise customers in the janitorial and sanitation (jan/san) industry. Utilizing the Web, Cleanwise helps all participants in the jan/san industry realize greater efficiency, control,and cost savings. Through its Cleanwise.com site and eStores, Cleanwise provides the industry's only complete source for the procurement of jan/san products, services, and training.

Based in Marlborough, Mass., Cleanwise was founded by jan/san industry veterans in December 1999 and is backed by Insight Capital Partners and Johnson Wax Professional.
